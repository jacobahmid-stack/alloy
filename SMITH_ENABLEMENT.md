# Smith Enablement: teaching partners to sell the AWS way

Onboarding + continuous, delivered by Smith on the partner's own live pipeline. Design + verified canon + build spec. 2026-07-17.

## Why this is the activation unlock

Activation is stuck because the partner does not know the game, not because Smith is missing a feature. Alto and Novalo are competent operators who have never sold the AWS way. They do not know that "Committed" is not a signature, that you cannot submit a Prospect into ACE, that MAP pays against milestones rather than upfront, or that "do nothing" is the competitor they actually have to beat. So when Smith hands them a fundable play, they freeze, because acting on it means speaking a language they were never taught. Enablement removes that freeze. It has to be Smith-delivered and embedded in real pipeline: a static LMS teaches MEDDPICC on a fictional deal and is forgotten by Friday, whereas Smith teaches it on the rep's own top account, names the one gap on that real deal, and turns the lesson into the next move.

The one-liner: **Smith does not run a course. Smith teaches you AWS selling on your own live deal, then helps you win it.** Enablement is the purest form of "I forge it, you close it": it makes the human permanently better instead of doing their job for them.

## The corrections the verification caught (encode these, do not teach the old version)

1. **POD is retired.** The Partner Originated Discount (POD) was retired 1 Jan 2026 and folded, with two other incentives, into a single **New Customer Incentive**. Teach the motion (partner-originated new-customer wins, submitted and Launched through ACE); name the New Customer Incentive; never quote POD.
2. **"Committed" is not a signature.** AWS's exact definition: "the customer has agreed to the solution and commits to moving with technology, architecture, and economics." Only **Launched** = billing begun, and only Launched earns tier credit and most funding.
3. **There is no official "AWS MEDDICC."** MEDDICC/MEDDPICC is the industry framework the AWS ecosystem adopted as shared qualification language. AWS's own construct is the ACE stage ladder + an Opportunity Quality score + the co-sell motion. Teach it as "how AWS-ecosystem sellers qualify," never as an AWS product.
4. **STAR is DDI (1974), not Amazon.** Amazon popularized it; do not call it "the Amazon STAR method."
5. **Never assert soft figures.** MAP percentage (~15-25% of first-year run-rate), POC (~$25K), MDF (~$5K/$10K, ~50%), Marketplace fee (1.5-3%), any PRM deadline: all VERIFY LIVE, deferred to Partner Central. The APN/tier annual fee of $2,500 IS confirmed.

Everything else load-bearing was confirmed against primary sources: all acronym expansions, Working Backwards steps, the 7 Rs, the 6 Well-Architected pillars, the 3 FTR pillars + 3-year validity + Qualified Software badge + SOC2/WAFR waiver, the 5 Partner Paths, Select/Advanced/Premier thresholds, tiers-are-Services-Path-only, badges-for-ISVs, "cannot submit a Prospect," ISV Accelerate 3%->1.5%, and the 16 Leadership Principles with Customer Obsession first.

## The two modes

### Onboarding path (first week, one module per working day, ~10-15 min each)

Each module = a short teach from the verified canon, a "you can now do X" outcome, and a real Alloy action it graduates into, so the lesson lands on the rep's own book.

| # | Module | Teaches | You can now | Graduates into |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Calibrate | 3 questions on AWS-selling experience + known methods; writes `experience`/`known_methods` on the rulebook | have Smith right-size every answer | onboarding step 3; sets the calibration Smith reads on every coach reply |
| 1 | The motion | co-sell + ACE; partner-originated new-customer is the highest-value motion; the wedge is the layer before Partner Central | explain why you bring AWS a net-new account and why "do nothing" is the real competitor | open the Letter or `/score` your top account (one all-cloud fundable account) |
| 2 | Decode the language | ACE ladder, tiers vs badges, the 7 Rs, TAM/SAM/SOM, ARR/MRR, TCO, landing zone | read an AWS deal without a translator | research one account with the company read (`smith-read`) |
| 3 | Qualify a real deal | MEDDPICC mapped onto the ACE stages | tell whether a deal is real and name the one gap | `/coach` your top open account: MEDDPICC, the one gap for this week |
| 4 | The funded plays | MAP phases, POC funding, MDF, the FTR gate, ISV Accelerate, the 6 pillars | name the AWS program that funds this customer's next move | run funding-fit / MAP Assess kit on a live account |
| 5 | Write it the AWS way | Working Backwards (PR/FAQ), STAR with a quantified Result, Customer Obsession, two-way-door framing | write a co-sell narrative AWS will act on | the migration-assessment / funding-paperwork kit |

The rep finishes the week having qualified a real deal, sized a funded play, and drafted a real narrative. The onboarding path *is* first activation.

## The seed cards (paste-ready, Smith's voice, KB `kind:"method"` docs)

Each card: short teach, acronym expanded once, source cited, ends with an "apply it to YOUR account now" prompt. VERIFY-LIVE figures are never asserted.

**Working Backwards.** Amazon's method for pressure-testing an idea by starting from the finished customer experience and reasoning back. The artifact is a PR/FAQ (a one-page Press Release plus FAQ), written as if it already shipped, before you build. Five customer questions: who is the customer, what is their problem, the single most important benefit, how you know they want it, what the experience looks like. Maps one-to-one to a co-sell narrative and the ACE customer-business-problem field. Mistake: writing the press release about your own features. It is a customer-outcome document, never sent to the customer. Apply it now: pick a net-new account still on another cloud and I will Working-Backwards a one-page narrative, before it exists in Partner Central. [workingbackwards.com]

**STAR.** Tell a story so the claim lands with evidence: Situation, Task, Action (the weight-bearing part), Result (quantified). The shape of every co-sell reference. Two flags: STAR is DDI (1974), not Amazon; and the classic failure is stopping at Action. "It went well" is not a Result. "Three workloads migrated, 28 percent run-rate cut, live in six weeks" is. Apply it now: give me a win and I will rebuild it as a STAR proof with the Result quantified. [ddi.com]

**MEDDPICC.** How AWS-ecosystem sellers qualify: Metrics (value in money), Economic buyer (owns the budget), Decision criteria (what you are scored on), Decision process (steps to yes), Paper process (legal/security/procurement between verbal yes and signature), Identify pain (what forces action), Champion (an insider with power who sells for you when you are away), Competition (rival, internal build, or "do nothing"). A friendly contact is not a Champion. On cloud deals the Paper process is where "closed" deals die. In the AWS motion, "stay on the current cloud" IS the Competition. Flag: no AWS product called "MEDDICC"; AWS's own construct is the ACE ladder + Opportunity Quality score. Apply it now: name your top open account and the eight; I will show which letters are filled, which are guesses, and the one gap for this week. [meddicc.com]

**Co-sell + ACE.** You bring the solution and expertise; AWS brings the field seller, the account relationship, and funding. ACE is where you register and manage co-sell opportunities. In ACE or invisible: a deal not in ACE cannot be funded, staffed, or attributed. Mindset flip: co-sell is not AWS handing you leads, it is the reverse. Partner-originated net-new is what AWS rewards most, which is our wedge. (ACE-eligible takes 15 validated opportunities.) Apply it now: pick a net-new account and I will draft the ACE opportunity, staged correctly. [aws.amazon.com/partners/programs/ace]

**The ACE stage ladder.** Prospect (identified) -> Qualified (customer agrees it is real; minimum bar to submit) -> Technical validation (architecture/POC) -> Business validation (money agreed, pricing proposed) -> Committed (customer commits to move on technology, architecture and economics) -> Launched (billing begun; earns tier credit, attribution, most funding) -> Closed lost. You cannot submit a Prospect. Committed is not a signature or billing; only Launched earns. Never inflate a stage; it breaks trust with your AWS seller. Apply it now: tell me where a live deal sits and I will pressure-test the stage and name what must be true to move it up one. [docs.aws.amazon.com partner-central sales-guide]

**Origination + the New Customer Incentive.** Every ACE opportunity is Partner-Originated or AWS-Originated. Partner-originated net-new is our whole wedge. As of 1 Jan 2026 the old POD is retired and folded into a single New Customer Incentive across the Solution Provider and Distribution programs. The motion is unchanged; only the name changed. Do not wait for AWS-originated leads, and do not quote POD. Current terms live in Partner Central. [aws.amazon.com/blogs/apn updates-2026]

**MAP.** AWS's flagship program to fund and de-risk moving existing workloads. Three phases: Assess (estate, TCO, business case), Mobilize (landing zone, security baseline, skills, plan), Migrate and Modernize (execute). Earned against milestones and consumption, not upfront, and not enterprise-only (Lite lanes exist). The credit is a percentage of first-year run-rate, deal-specific, so I say "a percentage of run-rate" and we confirm the live figure in Partner Central. Apply it now: give me an account running an existing estate elsewhere and I will draft the MAP Assess business case. [aws.amazon.com/migration-acceleration-program]

**POC funding + MDF.** POC funding pays for a small pre-commitment pilot for a customer not yet on AWS; it precedes the bigger MAP motion and is the bankable form of a net-new all-cloud play. MDF reimburses approved demand-gen tied to AWS solutions, usually claim-and-reimburse, often gated behind a Competency/Service Ready. Sizes move often, so every figure is VERIFY LIVE in the Partner Funding portal. Apply it now: name a customer not yet on AWS and I will size whether a funded POC is the right first step. [aws.amazon.com/partners/funding]

**Tiers vs badges + the FTR.** Tiers (Select, Advanced, Premier) are Services-path only, earned on launched opportunities at rising MRR bars (APN fee $2,500/yr). Software/hardware partners earn badges instead. The first is Qualified Software, via the FTR: a free self-service review of your solution against three Well-Architected pillars (security, reliability, operational excellence), valid three years, waivable by SOC 2 Type II or a Well-Architected Review with AWS. Passing gates funding, the Competency program, and ISV Accelerate. An ISV asking "how do we reach Advanced?" is asking the wrong question. Apply it now: tell me your partner path and I will map the exact next badge or tier and what it unlocks. [aws.amazon.com/partners/services-tiers]

**The 7 Rs.** Rehost, Replatform, Refactor/Re-architect, Repurchase, Retire, Retain, Relocate. The R per workload sets effort, risk, funding lane, and timeline. Mistake: assuming everything must be Refactored; AWS recommends against refactoring during a large migration, favoring rehost/replatform/relocate first, then modernizing after. Apply it now: give me a workload and I will propose its R, the reason, and the funding lane it opens. [docs.aws.amazon.com prescriptive-guidance]

**The six Well-Architected pillars.** Operational Excellence, Security, Reliability, Performance Efficiency, Cost Optimization, Sustainability. A Well-Architected Review applies them to a customer workload, surfaces High Risk Issues, and carries remediation funding. Not theory: a paid, repeatable reason to be in the account. Apply it now: name a customer workload and I will flag the pillars most at risk and the funded review that fits. [docs.aws.amazon.com wellarchitected]

**Vocabulary quick-cards.** Landing zone = a governed multi-account AWS foundation, the main deliverable of MAP Mobilize, not "just an account." Marketplace / Private Offer / CPPO = the catalog a customer buys onto their AWS bill; a Private Offer is a negotiated non-public deal; a CPPO adds a reseller who owns billing while the ISV sets margin. Attribution / PRM = how AWS ties a customer's spend back to your deal; no ACE + no PRM = invisible to the incentive engine (any hard date is VERIFY LIVE). Sizing = TAM/SAM/SOM (AWS cares about near-term obtainable run-rate); ARR/MRR (recurring AWS consumption, not your invoice); TCO (like-for-like, the backbone of the MAP Assess case). Culture cues = Customer Obsession is LP one of sixteen; a two-way door is reversible (decide fast at ~70% info), and framing a funded POC or MAP Assess as a cheap two-way door unsticks a hesitant buyer.

## Build spec (minimal, on the existing stack)

**Cards live in the KB, not a new table.** Author them as `kb_docs` with `kind:"method"`. The retrieval path already carries `kind` (`kb-search/index.ts:69`); ingest is `service_role`/cron-gated (`kb-ingest/index.ts:22-23`) so no tenant can inject a card (this is the "teach only the verified set" guarantee); hybrid retrieval already fuses summary + detail (`match_kb_chunks` + `search_kb_fts`, RRF). Add the method acronyms to `KB_SYNONYMS` (`kb-search/index.ts:20`) so "economic buyer" cross-hits MEDDPICC. One line in `box/KB_INGEST_CHECKLIST.md` to admit sales methodology.

**Prompt/tool hooks.** `/learn <topic>` = retrieval-only (one `SHORTCUTS` entry, one `registry.js` group, one `router.js` intent, one `runShortcut` branch calling `kbSearch()` filtered to `kind:"method"`) - no Claude call, deterministic cited card. `/coach` = one grounded Claude call (reuses `/forge`/`smithChat` grounding + a framework lens; new `coach` task in `claude-proxy` beside `next_best_action`; grounds against the method cards so Smith teaches from canon). JIT "method behind this" = deterministic strings on the NBA strip, the Letter P.S., and the brief, keyed to the day's top play via a static play-to-method table.

**Schema.** Ship early: one `experience` (+ optional `known_methods[]`) field on `gtm_rulebook`, rendered in `formatRulebook` (keep the claude-proxy mirror in sync). Defer: a small `enablement_progress` table, only if a visible checklist is wanted.

**Spend.** Teaching is retrieval, not generation. `/learn` and every JIT line spend nothing. Only `/coach` calls Claude, once, gated by lean-mode + the global cap + per-tenant caps. Barely moves COGS; the accuracy-critical cards never cost a token to serve. Naming: `/learn` and `/coach` are plain verbs, zero persona-budget; never brand the layer ("Academy", "Apprenticeship" violate the cap).

## First slice (build now)

**The seed method cards in the KB + `/learn` (retrieval-only).** This is the accuracy firewall the whole layer depends on: one source-cited card, authored once and reused everywhere, beats Smith improvising a framework per chat. It proves the teach half of the loop end to end and is the safest thing to ship: additive data + an additive slash command, no existing behavior changed, zero Claude spend, no schema, no persona-budget. Rollback = delete the `kb_docs` method rows and revert a small additive diff. Files: `kb-ingest` (author cards), `kb-search:20` (synonyms), `shortcuts.js:16`, `registry.js:35`, `router.js:52`, `forge.jsx:7794` (the `/learn` branch). The immediate next slice, same week, is `/coach` on the open account, which closes the loop into action on the rep's own live deal.

## Risks

- **Teaching something wrong.** A partner acts on what Smith teaches. Mitigation: the verified card set is the only source; ingest is service_role-gated; `/coach` grounds against the cards; the known correction traps are pinned in the cards (New Customer Incentive not POD; Committed is not a signature; MEDDPICC not "AWS MEDDICC"; STAR is DDI; every soft figure VERIFY LIVE).
- **Scope creep into a real LMS.** Hold the line at retrieval + one grounded coach call + deterministic JIT lines. Six short steps that each graduate into a real Alloy action, not a syllabus. No grades, no certificates.
- **Drifting from amplify to replace.** Every card and coach reply ends in a "you do X now" prompt on the rep's own account. Smith names the gap and the method; the human asks the question, makes the call, and sends everything.
