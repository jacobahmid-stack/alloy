# Forged Light

A visual philosophy for Forj and Alloy.

**The forge and the metal.** Two surfaces, one fire. Forj is the dark smithy: warm near-black, a single ember glow rising from the lower corner, the cream mark struck clean into it. Alloy is the metal pulled from that fire: the same mark, now cut into a sheet of molten gradient, rust deepening to ember to bright edge-light. One says where the work is made; the other says what comes out of it. They are unmistakably a family, distinguished only by which side of the heat they stand on.

**Restraint as luxury.** Nordic-understated. Vast calm fields of warm dark, broken by one deliberate gesture of light and one line of type. Nothing decorative, nothing loud. The confidence is in what is left out. Every margin is generous; the eye is given room to rest before it reads.

**Type with two voices.** A serif display (DM Serif Display) carries the single human line, the tagline, set large and quiet, with the operative word lit in molten gradient. A grotesque (Bricolage) handles the small letterspaced wordmark, machined and precise. The contrast of the warm serif and the engineered grotesque is the contrast of craft and system, which is the whole proposition.

**Master-level execution.** Every value is the exact brand value, every edge is crisp, the ember glow is calibrated so it warms without smudging, the mark sits at its true geometry. This should read as the product of painstaking attention, work that took countless hours, made by someone at the top of the craft. Pristine, balanced, and quietly certain of itself.
