# Positioning: Smith is a loop-engineered GTM system

The angle, riding the current "loop engineering" wave (Boris Cherny / Addy Osmani / the viral
threads): most sales AI is a chatbot you prompt. Smith is a loop that runs the work and gets sharper
every cycle. This is a sharper story than "AI co-worker" and it is true to what Smith actually is.

Guardrails for all copy here: no em dashes (commas, colons, periods). Cloud-agnostic (the funded play
ON their cloud, never "move off it"). No partner names, no contact names. Customer-facing surfaces
(forj.se) stay at the OUTCOME level, never the backend (no tables, models, or mechanics).

## The one-liner
Most sales AI is a chatbot you prompt. Smith is a loop that works your pipeline and gets sharper every
week. Smith forges it. You close it.

## The message ladder
1. **You were the loop.** For two years, AI meant: type a prompt, read the answer, type the next one.
   You sat inside the loop the whole time.
2. **Smith takes you out of it.** It watches the market for the signal, names the funded play that
   fits, drafts the open, and works the account forward. You stop driving every touch.
3. **It compounds.** A chatbot is the same on day 100 as day 1. Smith gets better every cycle, because
   every deal it works teaches the next one. The win your rep closed Monday sharpens every account
   Smith touches Tuesday.
4. **Grounded, on your cloud.** Across AWS, Azure and Google Cloud, on real funding programs and real
   buying signals, not guesses. That grounding is the moat: anyone can bolt a chatbot on; the signal
   layer and the compounding are what they cannot copy.

## LinkedIn post (ready to publish, Jacob's voice)

Two years ago, working with AI meant typing a prompt, reading the answer, typing the next one. You were the loop.

The best engineers stopped doing that. Boris Cherny, who runs Claude Code, put it plainly: "My job is to write loops." You stop sitting inside the loop. You design it once, and it runs, verifies its own work, and keeps going.

I built Smith on that idea, for sales.

Most sales AI is still a chatbot. You prompt it, it answers, you prompt again. You are the loop.

Smith is not. It watches the market for the signal, names the funded play that fits the account, drafts the open, and records what actually worked. Then it does it again, sharper. The deal your rep closed on Monday makes every account Smith touches on Tuesday a little better.

That is the part that compounds. A chatbot is the same on day 100 as it was on day 1. A loop gets better every cycle, because every cycle writes a lesson back.

Across AWS, Azure and Google Cloud. Grounded in real funding programs and real signals, not guesses.

Smith forges it. You close it.
