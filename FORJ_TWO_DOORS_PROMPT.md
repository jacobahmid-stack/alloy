# FORJ: THE TWO DOORS

**The prompt for the 2026 brand, forj.se, and the commercial spine.**
Written 2026-07-18. Every number here was measured this week, not estimated.

---

## THE SPINE

> **The Desk proves the territory with our humans. Alloy runs the same machine with yours.**

One sentence, two clauses, and the whole business sits in the join between them.

Read it slowly, because it makes three claims and only two of them are currently true.

1. **"The Desk proves the territory"** — TRUE, measured. Jacob works a partner's market personally and produces qualified meetings with a funded AWS play attached.
2. **"the same machine"** — TRUE, structural. One library, one funded-play engine, one Smith. The Desk is not a separate service running on separate rails. Forj's own people use exactly what a customer gets.
3. **"with yours"** — **NOT YET PROVEN.** Partner Smith usage was ZERO for the entire operating period until attribution was fixed on 2026-07-15. Two customers pay for software. Neither had worked the machine themselves.

**That asymmetry is the single most important fact in this brief, and every piece of work built on this spine must respect it.** The line is a promise about what the product is for, not a claim about what customers have already done. Until a partner's own rep books a meeting off Smith's brief, clause three is intent. Write it as intent. It is still a strong and honest thing to say. It becomes a devastating thing to say the day it is evidenced, and that day is close.

---

## WHY THE SPINE MATTERS MORE THAN THE WORDS

It resolves four problems at once, which is why it is the spine and not a tagline.

**It resolves the identity question.** Forj is not a software company with a services arm, and not an agency with a tool. It is one machine with two sets of hands available to run it. That is a coherent category a buyer can hold in their head.

**It resolves the founder-dependence problem.** A founder-delivered service is a valuation problem and a life problem. A founder-delivered *demonstration of a product* is a go-to-market strategy with a designed end. Same activity, entirely different meaning. The Desk stops being what Forj is and becomes how Forj proves.

**It resolves the price ladder.** 100,000 SEK/month against 3,900 stops looking like a 25x gap between a premium tier and a toy. It becomes the honest price of labour: the machine costs the same, the humans are the variable. That reframe is why the ladder is defensible instead of embarrassing.

**It resolves graduation.** A Desk customer moving down to software is not churn or downgrade. It is the plan working. That must be true in the contract, not only in the story.

## THE MEASURED STATE, INCLUDING WHAT HURTS

Any work on this brief must be grounded in these, not in aspiration.

### The company
- Solo founder. Jacob does prospecting, calls, meetings, deals, product and data.
- Qubad: junior in AWS domain skills, **one meeting delivered in four weeks**.
- Platform launched **1 June 2026**. Vacation from 1 July. Back on the phones **10 August**. So: roughly one month of real operation.
- Two paying customers, both on software: Alto and Novalo. Alto has a live project going well. Long-term commitment conversations with both within weeks.

### The machine
- Library: **85,693 Nordic companies** (SE 30,079 / NO 35,458 / FI 19,941), cloud-read across all of them, 41.7k domained.
- Funded-play engine: deterministic, no LLM, maps every account to an AWS door.
- Smith: openly AI, daily brief, drafts and prepares. **Humans send everything.**
- Estate reads: positive DNS evidence of privately hosted business systems, with the incumbent provider named.

### The uncomfortable facts
- **Reachability is 99.3% Swedish.** Norway 129 contacted companies. Finland 31. Any Nordic-reach claim is false today.
- **97% of contacts and phone numbers came from a Vainu trial API of unverified licence status.** Until resolved, Forj must make no public claim about the provenance or ownership of its contact data, and must have an answer ready if a customer asks in a renewal conversation.
- **Partner Smith usage was zero until 2026-07-15.**
- AWS shipped opportunity drafting, scoring and enrichment first-party and free in July 2026, commoditising the assist layer. The one structural survivor: **AWS does no discovery.** Partner Central sees nothing until an account is already an opportunity. Forj's defensible ground is the layer *before* Partner Central.

## THE TWO DOORS, DEFINED

Both doors run the identical machine. Nothing is withheld from the software door to make the Desk look better, and nothing about that arrangement is a concession: it is the entire proposition.

### Door A: The Desk
Forj's own people work the partner's territory. 100,000 SEK/month, three-month block, roughly 50% co-funded through MDF where approved, with a contractual floor of 15 qualified meetings per block.

What the partner gets: meetings held, with a funded AWS play attached and the ACE paperwork prepared. What they keep afterwards: the pipeline.

### Door B: Your reps
The partner's own team works it, with Alloy and Smith. 3,900 SEK/month, 7,600 for Plus, flat, no per-contact metering, ever.

What the partner gets: the same library, the same funded-play reads, the same daily brief. What they keep afterwards: the pipeline **and the machine**.

### The honest comparison

Publish these rows. Every one is structural and true today.

| | We run it | Your reps run it |
|---|---|---|
| Who does the work | Forj's people | Your team |
| Cost per qualified meeting | roughly 5x | roughly 1x |
| Time to first meeting | days | weeks, plus ramp |
| Capacity ceiling | hard, and it is us | yours to expand |
| What you keep after | the pipeline | the pipeline and the machine |
| AWS program fluency | comes with us | has to transfer |

**That last row decides the comparison and is the one row without evidence.** Do not fake it. State it as what it is: the fluency arrives with the Desk, and the machine is built to hand it over. That is an honest promise. The moment a partner rep books off a Smith brief, this row becomes the strongest on the page.

## THE LAW

Non-negotiable. Any output breaking one of these is wrong regardless of quality.

**Truth**
- One month of operation and two customers cannot be dressed as more. No implied scale, no implied team, no implied Nordic reach.
- No claim of AWS partnership status, tier, endorsement or program membership that Forj does not hold.
- Never claim customers are doing something they are not doing. Clause three of the spine is intent until measured.
- Every published number must survive a hostile reading by an AWS partner development manager who knows this market.

**Confidentiality**
- Alto and Novalo never appear publicly. Nor does the Novalo end customer, nor Marc Hillander pilot numbers.
- In a small market, industry plus size plus region can identify a customer as surely as a name. Check combinations, not just names.

**Pricing**
- No per-meeting, per-lead or per-message price in public, ever. The lead-seller scar. The block price and the software ladder may be public; the unit economics may not.
- Flat pricing. No per-contact metering. The anti-token spine is a positioning commitment, not a billing preference.

**Cloud posture**
- AWS-first identity, any-cloud library. Never equal-thirds multicloud branding.
- Never frame a play as moving a customer off their current cloud. The funded play is always a new workload on their journey, in AWS's own program.
- Never AWS orange #FF9900. The sanctioned amber is anchored #D98A33. No AWS logos.

**Smith**
- Openly AI, always. Smith never pretends to be human and never sends anything. A human sends every message.
- Never scrape LinkedIn or any social network.

**Craft**
- No em dashes or en dashes in any copy.
- Brand is industrial-forge: muted steel-violet, material over glow, no decorative flourish.
- forj.se is outcomes only. No vendors, backends, methods or data sources named.

## WHAT THE WORK MUST PRODUCE

1. **The spine, expressed.** The hero line and the paragraph under it, usable verbatim on the site and said out loud on a cold call without embarrassment.
2. **Door names.** Short, repeatable, honest about who does the work. A buyer must be able to say them back after one read.
3. **The head-to-head**, on the structural rows above, with the fluency row phrased as intent.
4. **The site structure**, section by section: what leads, where the doors appear and how prominently, what proof sits with each, and what is deleted from today's site. Today's site under-sells the Desk, which is the only thing that has produced meetings and deals.
5. **The routing question.** The one question on a first call that puts a prospect in the right door, its follow-ups, and its disqualifiers. Selling the wrong door is how a guarantee becomes a liability and a reference turns negative.
6. **The graduation mechanic**, written so it is designed rather than hoped, and belonging in the Desk contract from day one.
7. **The 30-day Door B proof.** The instrumentation and milestones that convert clause three from intent to evidence.

---

## HOW THE WORK IS JUDGED

Acceptance tests. Run them before shipping anything.

- **The PDM test.** A Nordic AWS partner MD reads the page. Can they state, unprompted, what Forj does and which door is theirs? Is there any sentence they would challenge that Forj cannot defend with a measured fact?
- **The clause-three test.** Does any copy imply customers are already running the machine themselves? If yes, it fails.
- **The identification test.** Could a reader in the Swedish AWS ecosystem deduce Alto or Novalo from what is written, including from combinations of industry, size and region?
- **The cheap-door test.** Does Door B read as a toy next to Door A? If yes, the pricing story has not been made. Same machine, different hands.
- **The focus test.** Does offering both read as flexibility or as a company that has not decided what it is? If the latter, the spine is not doing its job and the copy is presenting two products instead of one machine.
- **The spoken test.** Read the hero aloud as the opening of a cold call. Does it sound like a person or like a website?

## THE TRAPS

Specific failure modes, each seen or narrowly avoided in this business already.

**Dressing the Desk as scale.** "Our team" when the team is one founder and a junior with one meeting. A partner MD will ask who is on the account, and the answer had better already be on the page.

**Letting Door B carry unproven proof.** The temptation to write "partners run their own territory with Smith every day" is enormous and it is currently false. Zero usage until three days ago.

**Making graduation sound like a downgrade.** If Door B is described as the cheaper option rather than the destination, the ladder reads as a discount and the Desk reads as the real product. Invert it: the Desk is the ramp, the machine is the product.

**Publishing the unit economics.** 5,000 to 10,000 SEK per meeting is a compelling number in a room and a disaster in print. It reframes Forj as a lead seller and invites unit-price negotiation forever.

**Nordic overclaim.** Three countries in the library is true. Three countries reachable is not. Sweden 8,064 contacted companies, Norway 129, Finland 31.

**Exploiting the founder story.** Libya, the wrestler father jailed under Gaddafi, "the story of my life: fighting". This is real and it is why the brand is called Forj. It becomes differentiation when it explains why this person built this thing and why they will not quit. It becomes exploitation the moment it is deployed as a conversion device on a pricing page. Use it where it explains, never where it sells.

**Answering the data-provenance question badly.** Until the Vainu trial licence is resolved, there is no safe public claim about where the contact data came from. Prepare the answer before 10 August, because it will be asked in a renewal conversation, not after one.

## THE ONE THING

If everything else waits, this does not: **get one partner rep booking meetings off Smith's briefs.**

It costs nothing, needs no new customer, and it is the same test as the Qubad question wearing different clothes. It converts the spine's third clause from a promise into a fact. It turns the head-to-head from an argument into a demonstration. It is the difference between an acquirer pricing a founder's calendar and an acquirer pricing a machine.

Everything in this brief is stronger the day it lands.
