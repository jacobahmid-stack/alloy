# AWS claim card, read before you dial

## 1. DO NOT SAY (5 false claims still in the corpus)

Ordered by how often they come up on a call.

**1. "AWS does no discovery. Partner Central sees nothing until an account is already an opportunity."** (FORJ_TWO_DOORS_PROMPT.md, also NEW_MONEY_THESIS.md)
Both halves are wrong. Partner Central has a Leads layer that sits BEFORE opportunities, AWS shares leads off its own signals (webinar sign-ups, campaign activity, solution inquiries), and since June/July 2026 it enriches and scores those leads with propensity and program eligibility.
SAY INSTEAD: "AWS only sources and scores accounts already in its own funnel, and its prospecting runs on leads you or AWS already hold. It does not go find net-new accounts that aren't touching AWS yet. That first discovery step is what Alloy does." Keep the wedge on prior-touch versus no-touch, never on "AWS does nothing."

**2. "MAP Lite covers $1-100K" or any MAP dollar lane / tier / threshold.** (NEW_MONEY_THESIS.md, SMITH_ENABLEMENT.md)
Inverted and unpublished. $100K is a floor, not a ceiling, and AWS publishes NO lanes, tiers, or dollar thresholds on its MAP page at all.
SAY INSTEAD: "MAP funding scales with deal size; AWS supports migrations up to $10M ARR through the streamlined Partner Central path. AWS publishes no tiers or phase percentages, so I confirm the number in Partner Central for the specific deal." Never quote a dollar lane.

**3. "Partner Central prospecting / drafting / scoring is FREE to every ACE partner."** (Forj_Raise_Narrative.md, FORJ_TWO_DOORS_PROMPT.md, NEW_MONEY_THESIS.md)
AWS published an ELIGIBILITY statement ("available to all ACE-eligible partners"), which is not a price. AWS never says "free."
SAY INSTEAD: "available to all ACE-eligible partners." If pressed on cost: "AWS publishes eligibility, not a price, so I don't represent it as free. The only thing AWS actually calls free is Marketing Central, a separate tool."

**4. "RA-ID must be submitted by the 7th of the month" (or any monthly RA/PRM submission deadline).** (implicated via FORJSE_COPY_PROMPT.md "RA-ID allocations reviewed monthly")
No such submission event exists. PRM revenue attribution is passive; there is no partner monthly deadline.
SAY INSTEAD: Drop the deadline entirely. "PRM attribution is passive plumbing; there's no monthly partner submission event." If you need the wedge, it's that attribution gates funding, not that there's a date to hit.

**5. "GenAI PoC and WMP: the PoC amount is credited against the subsequent WMP award."** (Alloy-AWS-Use-Case.md, section 8 of the letter)
This applies a MAP rule to the ISV WMP, which is a different program, and even the MAP version lives only in the gated Program Funding Guide. An AWS funding reviewer will catch this.
SAY INSTEAD: Pull this line from the letter. On a call, ask instead of assert: "My understanding is the PoC comes first and isn't stacked with the migration funding. Can you confirm how a GenAI PoC nets against a later ISV WMP award in the current Program Funding Guide?"

## 2. HEDGE OR AVOID (33 unsourced or over-scoped claims)

These read as facts in the corpus but are not primary-sourced. You do not currently know which of your "facts" these are. Hedge every one.

### Funding mechanics (the biggest risk on a funding/PDM call, a reviewer catches these)

**PoC is deducted from / cannot stack with / is sequential to MAP or WMP** (Alloy-AWS-Use-Case.md x4, Alloy-Smith-Playbook.md x2, NEW_MONEY_THESIS.md, FORJSE_COPY_PROMPT.md). The real rule (PoC not combined with MAP, PoC deducted from later MAP) lives ONLY in the gated Partner Funding Benefits Guide, is about MAP not WMP, and is not on any public AWS page. HEDGE: "PoC and MAP don't run on the same deal at once; you do the PoC first and it gets credited toward a later MAP award. That's from the Partner Funding Benefits Guide in Partner Central, not a public page." Drop WMP from that sentence entirely. Never present it as published AWS doctrine.

**Largest MAP tranche pays a percentage of first-year AWS run-rate** (Alloy-Smith-Playbook.md). AWS publishes no formula. HEDGE: "The biggest piece is sized against the AWS run-rate of the migrated workloads. AWS doesn't publish the percentage; treat any number as directional." Better: don't quote a percentage on a first call.

**GenAI PoC is roughly the lower of 10% of year-one spend or SOW cost (~$25K)** (Alloy-Smith-Playbook.md). The 10%/$25K figure is only on consultancy blogs. HEDGE: "My understanding is it runs around 10% of expected first-year spend, bounded by project cost, but AWS doesn't publish that. Directional, not an AWS figure."

**GenAI PoC requires a pre-approval EMAIL before work begins** (Alloy-Smith-Playbook.md). The rule is real, the mechanism is wrong. SAY: "pre-approval in Partner Central before any work" (the Fund Request hits the Pre-Approval stage). Not an "email." And it's generic Sell/PoC funding, not a named "GenAI PoC" program.

**MAP is tiered on projected ARR across three phases** (FORJSE_COPY_PROMPT.md, NEW_MONEY_THESIS.md). Phases are real; "tiered" is not AWS's word and implies a published per-phase schedule that doesn't exist. SAY: "three phases, Assess / Mobilize / Migrate and Modernize; funding scales with deal size up to $10M ARR. Not tiered, no per-phase percentages."

**POC cash on customer sign-off** (FORJSE_COPY_PROMPT.md). There is no customer-sign-off event. SAY: "cash is a reimbursement on project or milestone completion, paid against an invoice through Payee Central." "Credits up front" is fine.

**POC precedes MAP and never shares scope** (FORJSE_COPY_PROMPT.md, NEW_MONEY_THESIS.md). Practitioner logic, not an AWS rule. SAY: "In practice most partners run the POC first, then MAP, and they're funded separately. AWS doesn't publish that as a mandated order." Keep POC-first as YOUR recommendation.

**MDF claims within 30 days with itemized/third-party receipts** (Alloy-Smith-Playbook.md, FORJSE_COPY_PROMPT.md). The 30-day rule is TRUE but drops the real cap: it's 30 days after activity completion OR December 15 of that year, whichever comes first. "Itemized/third-party receipts" is NOT AWS wording (AWS says "actuals" and "claim attachments"). SAY the 30-day-or-Dec-15 line confidently; say "you back the claim with your actual-cost documentation," not "third-party receipts."

**ISV Accelerate gives a reduced Marketplace listing fee** (Alloy-Smith-Playbook.md). Half true. The co-sell-incentive half is verbatim AWS. The reduced-listing-fee half is NOT an ISV Accelerate benefit AWS publishes. SAY only: "account managers get incentives for co-selling via Marketplace Private Offers." Do not tie a fee break to the program.

**Activate Portfolio requires a paid SUPPORT plan** (Alloy-Smith-Playbook.md). Wrong word. It's a paid ACCOUNT (billing) plan, not an AWS Support plan. SAY: "your AWS account on a paid billing tier, not the free plan. You don't need to buy Developer or Business Support."

### Program mechanics and deadlines

**Partner Central "3.0" migration deadline was 30 June 2026** (FORJ_ICP.md, Forj_Raise_Narrative.md). AWS publishes NO date and never uses "3.0" or "mandatory." SAY: "AWS moved Partner Central into the Console; partners have to migrate to keep co-sell/ACE/Marketplace. June 30 is quoted around the ecosystem but isn't on AWS's pages, so the authoritative date is in your own migration widget." Drop "Partner Central 3.0" entirely, it's a vendor label that signals you're reading a consultancy blog.

**AWS shipped free opportunity drafting/scoring/enrichment in July 2026** (FORJ_TWO_DOORS_PROMPT.md, NEW_MONEY_THESIS.md). Wrong month and wrong "free." Drafting shipped May, scoring/enrichment June, only prospecting in July. SAY: "Over May to July 2026 AWS shipped drafting, scoring and enrichment natively, available to ACE-eligible partners." Not one July release, not "free."

**Drafting/scoring/RA-ID are free-for-ACE table stakes** (Forj_Raise_Narrative.md). Same eligibility-is-not-price trap. SAY "available to ACE-eligible partners," never "free," and don't price the passive RA-ID/PRM plumbing at all.

**Marketplace requires listings to reach Committed/Launched since 1 July 2026** (PLATFORM_GATE.md). Wrong scope. You need an associated SOLUTION, and a Partner Solution qualifies, so no Marketplace listing is required. SAY: "to advance a co-sell opp to Committed/Launched you attach at least one solution in Limited or Public status; a Partner Solution counts."

**"Readiness verdicts" shipped 15 June 2026** (PLATFORM_GATE.md). AWS never uses "readiness verdicts," and June 15 was lead enrichment. SAY: "co-sell readiness scoring shipped June 16 as the Opportunity Quality Score." Drop the June 15 label.

**ACE opportunities must be at Qualified+ to submit** (BACKLOG.md, SMITH_ENABLEMENT.md "cannot submit a Prospect"). Contradicted by AWS, which says submit EARLY, Prospect is fine. The only bar is do-not-submit at Launched or Closed Won. SAY: "AWS wants you to submit early, even at Prospect; qualification raises the Opportunity Quality Score and your odds of field co-sell, it's not a submission gate."

**FTR = "Free Tier for Revenue," a 3-year ladder rung** (BACKLOG.md). FTR is Foundational Technical Review, and an AWS partner will catch "Free Tier for Revenue" instantly. SAY: "The Foundational Technical Review is free to run and valid three years once approved; it unlocks funding, the Qualified Software badge, and gates Competency and ISV Accelerate. It's a prerequisite review, not a ladder step."

### Positioning nuances a sharp partner will correct

**AWS tools start from a lead already in your funnel** (AWS_PC_HEAD_TO_HEAD.md). AWS actively pushes net-new leads TO partners. SAY: "AWS acts on leads you upload and leads AWS hands you off its own signals; either way the customer already raised a hand. It does not build accounts from a national registry, that's Alloy." Keep the wedge on prior-touch, not "already yours."

**ACE requires opportunities "prospected by your firm"** (AI_NATIVE_SERVICES_READ.md). Not AWS wording. SAY: "opportunities are partner-originated or AWS-originated; yours logs as a Partner Referral and goes through a validation review up to five business days." Don't quote "prospected by your firm" as a named rule.

**Partner-originated new-customer is "the highest-value motion"** (SMITH_ENABLEMENT.md). Not AWS doctrine, and in AWS's own taxonomy "partner-led" is the LOWEST-engagement motion. SAY: "AWS pays a discount specifically for originating new customers." Keep any superlative as clearly your own read.

**Launched earns "most funding"** (SMITH_ENABLEMENT.md). Launched = billing begun and earns tier credit, both true. But much funding is pre-Launch by design (PoC, MDF, Innovation Sandbox, MAP milestones). SAY: "only Launched earns tier credit; what lands at Launch is the cash co-sell incentive, not most of the funding."
