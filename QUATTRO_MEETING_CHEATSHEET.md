# Quattro pitch — Erik & Emilio · notes

**In one line:** Quattro's tech is strong, but "connect anything to anything" is hard to sell. Aim it at one market — property back-office — let companies try it themselves, and Forj brings the customers.

**Open with:** *"You've built something strong. I want to show you a way to sell more of it — to one market, with customers I bring you."*

---

## The four slides
1. **Strong product, hard to sell** → aim it at property back-office, and sell it differently.
2. **Find → Try → Close** → Forj feeds the right companies → they try a free version (Quattro connects it) → Alto closes. The free test doesn't eat services revenue — it just finds customers cheaper.
3. **Where the money is** → free test → the gate (licence, modules, migration, support) → live in the customer's own AWS, a migration AWS funds. Customer owns their setup & data; Alto owns and licenses the platform.
4. **If they push back** → four risks, four answers (below).

---

## Questions they'll ask — plain answers
- **"Does the free version eat our services revenue?"**
  → No. It's just a cheaper way to find customers. The money is still at go-live, and you reach more companies.
- **"Can customers really set it up themselves?"**
  → For the simple part, yes — and you keep the hard integrations as paid work. Support 3 common systems first; everything else is a sales lead.
- **"Public bodies can't just buy this (LOU)?"**
  → Right. Start selling to **private landlords**. For municipal housing, the free version is an evaluation tool that feeds their procurement.
- **"Will it clash with our sales team?"**
  → No — it hands them leads, and they're credited on what closes.
- **"Who owns what?"**
  → The customer owns their setup and data, in their own AWS. Alto owns and licenses the platform. They can't take the platform; they keep their data.

---

## The ask
Support 3 systems first, and a **10–15 private-landlord pilot** — start with the ones already using Kundo. Forj brings the customers; Alto runs the pilot. We measure: tests that go live, and the cost to win a customer vs today.

## Proof to drop
Forj already found **17 property companies using Kundo** — Stena Fastigheter, Heimstaden, Wallenstam, John Mattson, and 13 more. They already pay for tenant tools; the back-office is next.

## Close
*"Forj finds them, Alto closes them, AWS funds the move. Let's run the pilot and measure it."*
