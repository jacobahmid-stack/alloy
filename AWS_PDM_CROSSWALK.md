## Risk rails (hard limits)

- **No endorsement, no affiliation.** The posting is public evidence of demand, not a relationship. Ban "AWS-backed", "endorsed by AWS", "AWS chose us", "in partnership with AWS". Say plainly whenever it could be inferred: Forj is an independent company, not part of AWS. Bedrock is a service any customer buys, not a blessing.
- **The subject of AWS's want is a human hire, not Smith.** Safe: "the posting proves AWS wants its PDMs using AI for the long tail, and Smith is built to be that tool." Never: "AWS is looking for Smith" or "AWS wants our product." Always frame as their mandate mapping to what Smith does.
- **Keep AWS's name, logo and this posting off the public site.** Per the forj.se redaction rule (outcomes only), the posting and quotes live in the private decks only. No verbatim ad in any public asset, no AWS trademarks implying association.
- **"Ready to submit in Partner Central, shaped to pass ACE" describes drafting, not privileged access.** Never let it slide into "AWS-integrated", "certified", or "official integration". Smith has only the access any partner has and prepares work for a human to review and submit.
- **Keep human-closes literal.** A human approves and sends every play. Smith never contacts partners autonomously and never acts or speaks on behalf of AWS.
- **Treat it as a public job ad.** No implied inside knowledge or special channel. Its power is that anyone could find it. Overclaiming a private tie both weakens the argument and creates trademark and misrepresentation exposure.
