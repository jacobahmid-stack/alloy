## How it maps to the next-generation partner motion

- **Act, do not analyze.** Alloy surfaces the patterns (which accounts just became ready, the play, the funding that fits) so the rep spends time acting, not building spreadsheets.
- **Co-built Partner Business Plans.** Smith drafts the plan from the partner's capabilities and the live market: whitespace, the target accounts, the joint GTM motion, the certifications to build, the funding ask, and the QBR cadence. The rep edits and owns it.
- **AI-informed C-suite conversations.** Smith finds the decision-maker, names the play and the funding in one line, and drafts the opener and the meeting brief.
- **GenAI-led transformation, not just migration.** Smith reads each account for AI and data readiness and frames the net-new GenAI play, not only the lift-and-shift.
- **Tri-party plays.** Every tool an account already runs is a co-sell signal. Smith connects the delivery partner, the ISV on the account's stack, and the distribution motion into one play that scales to mid-market.
- **See the patterns others miss.** Alloy reads the whole market (cloud, stack, company size, decision-makers, funding fit) and ranks by what is fundable now, across AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.

## Why it holds up

Everything Smith advises is grounded and on the record: real market data, live cloud documentation, real pricing, and a full action trace behind every answer. Smith advises and drafts; your team decides and closes. Nothing leaves without a human.
