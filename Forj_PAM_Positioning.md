# Forj: the partner agent for the new partner manager

*Positioning narrative for partner and investor conversations. Draft 2026-06-27.*
*Source for the category data: Omdia, "The Shifting Partner Account Manager Role" (June 2026), amplified by Omdia Chief Analyst Jay McBain.*

**Thesis.** The partner account manager role is being rebuilt around AI, co-sell, and multi-party deals. Forj builds the system that runs it: **Alloy**, the multicloud system of record, and **Smith**, the AI co-worker who does the partner work so the human can orchestrate the ecosystem and close.

**The category: RevAI.** Alloy is the RevAI layer for hyperscaler-partner GTM. When the cloud sells itself and demand outruns any team's capacity, the winner is not more closers, it is the system that routes the right account to the right partner with the funded play already attached, plus an AI co-worker, Smith, who does the readiness humans never reach so the people go deep where it counts. Alloy is the multicloud system of record that understands what it holds: which accounts are ready, which AWS, Azure or Google program pays for the move, who to call, what to say. It builds capacity instead of rationing it, and it scores on what compounds, funded and renewed revenue, not vanity pipeline.

---

## The shift, and why now

Omdia puts close to **1,000,000 partner account managers across 35,000 vendors** (also called CAMs, SAMs, PSMs, PDMs). Their four-decade scorecard, sourced revenue from transactional resell, is closing. Three forces are converging:

- **Go-to-market is collaborative.** PAMs now orchestrate co-sell, co-marketing, co-innovation, deal registration and joint pipeline, not a single resell relationship.
- **Consumption rewrites the scorecard.** Primary metrics move to net new and net ACV, measured against retention near 90 percent.
- **AI removes the admin.** Partner agents and AI portals strip the manual load, freeing the PAM for strategic work that was crowded out.

The PAM is becoming an **ecosystem revenue orchestrator**, accountable for outcomes across the whole customer journey.

## The gap

Vendors keep paying for transactions while recruiting for the role they are trying to leave. The daily work is still mechanical: finding the right accounts, shaping the co-sell, lining up the funding, tracking who touched the deal. Attribution across an average of **6.3 partners per customer** is unsolved. And the "AI tooling" the analysts describe barely exists yet.

## Where Forj sits

Forj is the AI tooling Omdia names. Every line of the modern PAM maps one to one:

- **AI frees the PAM** to do strategic work → **Smith** reads the market, shapes the funded play, and drafts the outreach. The human keeps the relationship and the close. Smith forges it, you close it.
- **Co-sell and multi-party alignment** → Smith shapes the co-sell and the tri-party play (partner + ISV + distribution), in each cloud's own program: AWS MAP, Azure Migrate and Modernize, Google RAMP.
- **Outcome-based orchestration** → **Alloy** is the system of record: every buying signal, the cloud each account runs, the stack on top, the size, the readiness, and the decision-maker, in one place, owned by the customer.
