THE VERDICT

The human look is a managed strength, not a liability: warmth is what makes honest AI disclosure affordable (the disclosure penalty vanishes at high social presence, and category backlash attaches to photorealism and deception, never to warm cartoons), and stylizing away would converge Smith with Andsend and surrender the moat. But the art itself carries zero non-human signal, and at 24 to 58px the illustration collapses into what reads as a stylized headshot of a real man, so the entire openly-AI law is carried by adjacent text. That text is currently concentrated in exactly two places (the index eyebrow and /smith) while the face travels everywhere, and it is absent on the most external, most legally exposed surfaces: the meeting bot (EU AI Act Art 50 applies 2026-08-02, 17 days out), the og share card, and the app. The single resolving principle: **the face never travels without the law in eyeshot.** Wherever the face or the name Smith appears, an AI marker (text within eyeshot, or a hallmark baked into the avatar under 64px and off-domain) must appear with it; the art stays warm, the disclosure moves from pages into pixels.
