THE PROCESS (run it as a workflow, not solo):
- SEE: screenshot the LIVE production surface, both themes, every viewport. Map the source (bands, tokens, type scale, JS behaviors, every image) with line numbers.
- AUDIT: five parallel lenses against the unicorn bar: first-impression gap, typography and layout craft, IA density and the kill list, copy tone (quote the weakest lines, rewrite them), production polish details. Every finding cites a band and a line.
- GATECHECK: one agent whose only job is to protect the laws. It kills any recommendation that violates a gate and confirms which ids and JS are load-bearing.
- DESIGN: three complete competing directions, each with exact copy and a full band list. Judge each with three independent judges on: unicorn, air, outcome, truth, feasibility, distinctive. Scores 1 to 10, be harsh.
- SPEC: the winner is the spine; graft the best ideas from the losers by name. Output one build spec with final copy (not suggestions), concrete CSS values, per-band notes on what survives and what dies, an image plan, the one motion moment, and a verification checklist. The builder executes it without asking questions.
- BUILD: rebuild in place, single file, porting the load-bearing JS and CSS verbatim with only the changes the spec names.
- VERIFY before shipping, all of it:
  grep gates: one smith.webp, one Amazon Bedrock, zero illustration refs, zero em or en dashes, zero qualif, zero FF9900, sr-* family alive, zero inline font-size or margin-top style attributes in markup.
  functional gates: free read end to end, all console chips play plus live chat, RPC numbers paint and hide honestly offline, theme flips both ways twice with the meta color, rail spy and deep links, reduced motion renders complete.
  visual gates: total height at 1440x900 of 7 viewports or less, visible words under 1,100, one H1, one pull-quote, both themes shot and eyeballed, zero console errors.
- SHIP: push, wait out the deploy, re-run the verification against production, and show the proof shots. Not done until production is verified.

TIER-DOWN TARGETS: 7 viewports max. Under 1,100 visible words. Every concept has exactly one home on the page. A zero never earns a slot. One numeral moment per screen. Fewer bands with more air beats more bands with less. When in doubt, cut the band and keep the sentence.
